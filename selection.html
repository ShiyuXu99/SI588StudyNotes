Fitts’s Law

Fitts’s Law tells us that larger, closer targets are easier for us to hit when pointing.

(The pointing time depending on the distance you are to the target and the size of the target)

  • Fitts’s Law: T = a + b*ID
    • a = intercept concept constant - how easy to start and stop moving
    • b = Slope constant - how hard it is to move and point

Larger objects = faster pointing

Shorter distances = faster reactions

Stimulus-Response Compatibility

Stimuli and responses are compatible when they are mapped in some meaningful way—e.g., they are the same color, their physical arrangements match. Well-mapped stimuli and responses mean slower selection times.

If Stimuli & Response are mapped, response time does notvary with number of options (n)

If S & R are not mapped, response time does vary with number of options (n)

Example: Button of the burners that matched the burner’s placement.

Hick’s Law

Hick’s Law tells us that the time it takes to choose a response is a function of how many choices we have.

Example: Amazon’s front page. Shouldn’t overwhelm user with too much choices on one page.

Design Implications

  • Broader and shallower menu are easier to choose.
  • Selection time is depended on the size of the button (size of the target)
  • Arrangement (placing things on a corner is a bad idea)
  • Sometimes by touch is faster by sight (like driving)
