  • Different media provide different “constraints on grounding”

What is Common Ground?

Something that people all know each other knows.

“a proposition p is only common ground if: all the people conversing know p; and they know that they all know p”

ex. Text on a graph explaining what things are.

  • Common language (e.g., English)
  • Common capabilities (e.g., sight, hearing)
  • Common reference (e.g., visual material)
  • Common knowledge (e.g., domain knowledge, common sense)
  • Common vocabulary (e.g., technical vs. lay terms)
  • Protocol (e.g., professional etiquette)
  • Culture (comes with assumptions about knowledge, protocol)
  • History (recent and distant)

ex. Menu of a new pages

Interpreting Language

Face to face is a joint action.

  • It’s “basic” that don’t need to lean
    • Precedes written communication
  • It’s not just verbal
    • Gesture, facial expression and grunts
  • It’s a joint action, two people have to do it together
  • F2F uses common ground to make communication efficient
  • F2F conversation develops common ground
  • k is a joint action engaged by A and B if…
    • A believes k includes A’s part plus B’s part
    • A intends to do A’s part
    • B believes k includes A’s part plus B’s part
    • B believes A intends to do A’s part
    • A believes B believes A intends to do A’s part

Constraints on Grounding

  • Copresence: A and B in same physical environment (colocation)
  • Visibility: A and B are visible to one another
  • Audibility: A and B communicate by speaking, noise
  • Contemporality: B receives at roughly the same time as A produces
  • Simultaneity: A and B can send/receive simultaneously (e.g., nodding)
  • Sequentiality: A’s and B’s turns cannot get out of sequence (e.g., emails out of order ~= sequential)
  • Reviewability: B can review A’s messages
  • Revisability: A can revise message for B (before they’re sent, like Slack or text or email)

Grice’s Maxims for Politeness

Quality: Things said should be true;

Quantity: Should contribute no more and no less that what conversation demands;

Relevance: Should be clearly related to purpose of conversation

Clarity: Contribution to a conversation should not be obscure
