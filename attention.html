Attention

#SI588

We don’t perceive everything we encounter, so what are the things that we will pay attention to?

Four Factors influence what we select to attend to:

  • Salience - How much a stimuli stand out
  • Expectancy - We tend to look at things we expected to be there
  • Value - Pay-out to finding the stimuli we need.
  • Effort - How difficult it is to find a stimuli.

Ex. Stop signs are red

Attention Process

Three Perceptual Process

Bottom-up Processing

physical makeup of stimulus, driven by salience;

Top-Down processing

Based on knowledge and contact, driven by expectancy and value

Unitization - Combined of the two process above

Example:

Image with words all messed up.

Top down processing help us guess what the content is, it’s driven by previous knowledge of words. It allows up understand things in different ways.

Eye Movement: Visual Search

Two type of Movement

Pursuit - Tracking a moving object. (Red dot across the screen)

Saccadic - Abrupt motions from one spot to another. (Recognize who the person is from her face), it also help us determined what order people will use when scanning a website.

Saccades

  • Initiation Latency - Amount of time take to initiate a saccade
  • Movement Time - Time for move from one spot to the next
  • Destination - point of fixation (most of our time during eye movement)
    • time/duration - how easily people can interpret the picture, extract things take more time
    • Useful field of view - people can grab lots of info on one glance

Visual Search

Salience

Desirable when you want people to find the target.

Expectations

People already have expectations for where to find things (Like the log out button, or the place of Michigan in a drop-down menu)

Signal Detection Theory

There are two state - Target present/absent

Discrimination & Absolute Judgement

Confusion occurs when two stimuli are very similar to each others.

Absolute refers to our ability to judge the absolute value of a variable. People are often not good at it.

Visual queries - Driving eye movements

We conduct visual queries in multiple loops. Outer, middle and inner loop. ex. Outer will find fruit, middle will began to look for fruit, inner will distinguish if it is a fruit.

Visual Design Tips

  • For bottom-up - Designs need to be clear and non ambiguous.
  • Should use things that people are already familiar to., consistency is the key.
  • Understand the user and know what they want to look at (use expectations)
