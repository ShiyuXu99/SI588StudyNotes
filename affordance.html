Designing tasks & systems: tension between situation awareness and

performance

Situation awareness is not related to performance.

Tips for Design a system to improve situation awareness:

  • Help people notice changes.
  • Make the situation easy to understand.
  • Keep the operator in the loop.
  • Help people project the future state of the system.
  • Organize information around goals.
  • Display to broaden attention.

Problem shooting & Trouble shooting

Problem Solving Approaches: Trial and error, luck, roubleshooting (requires mental model)

Trouble shooting is a part of problem solving.

Both impose cognitive burdens.

How to evaluate?

Events VS Non events.

Failures:

  • Cognitive tunneling – Occurs when you become fixated on one solution, even if it isn’t working.
  • Confirmation bias – Tendency to favor information that confirms beliefs or hypotheses.

Planning and Scheduling

  • Future can be broken into predicted states vs. command (desired) states.
  • Mental simulation: running the mental model to determine predicted state.
  • People tend to avoid complex, long range planning.
  • Automation tools can aid in planning/scheduling

Metacognition and Effort

Metacognition: knowing what you know

Anticipated effort: weighing the cost/benefit of taking action

(It’s possible to be wrong on both)

Don Norman: Good Design

Two characteristics of good design:

  • Understanding
  • Discoverability
    • Affordances - A relationship between “the world and an actor”
      • Not inherent to an object.
      • There can also be anti-affordances.
      • Affordances must be discoverable.
      • Affordances exist even if not visible.
    • Signifiers - A signifier communicates to a user where an action should take place.
      • Deliberate
      • Accidental/unintentional
    • Constraints
    • Mappings - The relationship between two things (controls their results in the world.
    • Feedback - Feedback communication of a system to a user
